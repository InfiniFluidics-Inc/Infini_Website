Traditional microfluidics has a scaling problem.

In conventional microfluidic manufacturing, each production unit needs its own pump, its own tubing, its own connections. Like early telephone switchboards, every new line requires manual wiring.

Early switchboards required a physical connection for every call. Conventional microfluidics works the same way.

Traditional scale-up means more hardware.

Each additional unit needs its own pump and plumbing. Complexity, cost, and failure points grow with every step.
